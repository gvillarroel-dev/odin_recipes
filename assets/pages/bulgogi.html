How to cook a Bulgogi?

Description

Bulgogi is a traditional Korean dish made with marinated beef that is grilled or stir-fried. The name "bulgogi" translates to "fire meat," which refers to the cooking method. This dish is known for its sweet and savory flavor, making it a favorite among many.

This recipe will guide you through making a delicious bulgogi at home. It's perfect for a family dinner or a special occasion!

Ingredients

  • 1 lb (450g) beef (ribeye or sirloin), thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 onion, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup green onions, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon sesame seeds
  • Black pepper to taste
  • Vegetable oil for cooking
  • Cooked rice for serving
  • Optional: lettuce leaves for wrapping

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, mix soy sauce, sugar, sesame oil, minced garlic, sliced onion, green onions, sesame seeds, and black pepper to create the marinade.
  2. Add the thinly sliced beef to the marinade. Mix well and let it marinate for at least 30 minutes (or overnight in the refrigerator for better flavor).
  3. Heat a skillet or grill over medium-high heat. Add a little vegetable oil to prevent sticking.
  4. Add the marinated beef to the skillet and cook until browned and cooked through, about 5-7 minutes.
  5. Serve the bulgogi with cooked rice and optional lettuce leaves for wrapping.
  6. Enjoy your delicious homemade bulgogi!

Now enjoy your Bulgogi!
